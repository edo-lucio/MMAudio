\title{Relational Cross-Modal Alignment via Gromov--Wasserstein\\
  Regularization for Video-to-Audio Flow Matching}
\author{Edoardo Luciani}
\date{\today}

\begin{document}
\maketitle
\begin{abstract}
Video-to-audio (V2A) flow-matching models such as MMAudio align
instance-level features through contrastive pairing, cross-attention,
and adaptive-LayerNorm conditioning. These are \emph{pointwise}
mechanisms: each video feature is matched to one audio feature.
The pairwise geometry of the two modalities --- which clips sound
alike, which look alike --- is not explicitly constrained. We add
an entropic Gromov--Wasserstein (GW) penalty between the
intra-video and intra-audio squared-distance matrices of every
mini-batch, solved via Peyr\'e-style mirror descent with an inner
Sinkhorn projection in float32. The regularizer drops into the
MMAudio training loop as one extra loss term with five knobs: the
representation pair it compares (four variants), its weight
$\lambda_{\mathrm{GW}}$, whether the video side is
gradient-detached, a warm-up schedule, and an interaction with
Synchformer features. We evaluate each axis on VGGSound against
FD$_{\text{PaSST}}$, Inception Score, ImageBind score, and DeSync,
plus a held-out-class OOD split and coupling-matrix diagnostics.
\end{abstract}
